About Persona

Persona is a private, offline-first tool designed for writers, actors, and creative professionals who want to build fictional characters with depth and authenticity.

Built entirely in your browser, Persona keeps your data safe on your device. We never see or store your work. All character information—from psychological profiles to relationship networks—lives locally, giving you complete privacy while you create.

Whether you're developing a complex cast for a novel, preparing character backstories for a film, or simply exploring the emotional arcs of fictional people, Persona helps you track the details that bring characters to life. Map psychological journeys across three acts, visualize relationships, and export comprehensive character bibles for your creative projects.

We believe great characters deserve great tools. Persona is free to use, and we're committed to keeping it that way.

🔒 Your data is stored locally - remember to back up your work!

Help & FAQ

How do I add characters?

Click the "+ Add Character" button in the header to open the character form. Fill in the name, backstory, and psychological profile details. Click Save to add the character to your project.

How do relationships work?

Open any character's details and go to the Relationships tab. Click "+ Add Relationship" to link other characters. Define the relationship type (e.g., friend, enemy, family) to map connections between your characters.

How do I export my data?

Use the Export button in the header to export your entire character bible as JSON or CSV. You can also generate a printable Character Bible PDF for sharing or printing.

Is my data private?

Yes. Persona is completely offline-first. All your data is stored locally in your browser's storage. We never see or transmit any of your work. Nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

What are Psychological Profiles?

Each character has a psychological profile based on Self Esteem (how they see themselves) versus Social Reputation (how the world sees them). This difference creates their Psychological Index—a value between -5 and +5 that tracks their emotional journey across three narrative acts.

A high positive index means high self-esteem with low social reputation (a misunderstood hero). A low negative index means low self-esteem with high social reputation (a respected but insecure figure). The index tracks how this gap narrows or widens throughout your story.
